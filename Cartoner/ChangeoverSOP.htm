Trash Bag Cartoner Changeover SOP

1.       Change the access level to service in HMI by performing following.

a.       On the main screen, click SETUP. On the set up screen, click ACCESS and then on the Access screen click on the right hand bottom screen with *****. This will bring a window to enter numbers. Select 2 and then press enter. This will change the screen color from green to orange and which means the machine is in service mode. Green screen indicates operator level access and the password to set this level is 1.

 

2.       Changing the product numbers in HMI.

a.       Cartoner has been programmed for 3 different sizes of cartons we run on the machine: 6 ct, 15 ct and 45 ct. And they are indicated by numbers 1, 2 and 3 respectively. These numbers will have to be changed based on type of cartons that needs to be set.

b.       Go to SETUP SCREEN as indicated under #1 above. Press PRODUCT button which will lead to PRODUCT SCREEN as below. Press on the BOX# with ** (usually it is set at 1, 2 or 3) and enter proper carton number that is being set and press enter. Go back to main screen.

3.       Adjusting the magazine holder (Accumulator)

a.       Remove any carton on the magazine holder and add only 1 carton as shown in the 2nd picture below. Each carton is held in a certain orientation on the magazine holder, make sure to use the correct orientation (see below for orientation of 3 different cartons when looking from back of the magazine holder).

 

b.       One of the carton holding base plate has a mark as shown below. This mark should be used to align with one side of carton flap bending edge as shown in picture below.

 

 

c.       Aligning the carton edge with the mark, loosen the two locking handles shown below and slightly tap both blocks (underneath the locking handles) of the magazine holder until inside face of the holders are almost touching the outside edges of the carton at both ends. There should be a slight play when the carton is moved side by side. Once set, lock both the handles again.

 

Bottom plate which holds the base of the carton should be adjusted (if necessary, shown above) such that it supports the edge just enough to hold the carton. This plate should never protrude out more than necessary since it will give issues during removal of the carton later.

 

d.       Adjust the holders which supports the top edge of the carton, shown in pictures below. There are locking handles on both sides, and needs to be adjusted based on the size of the carton. Two slotted plate supports (vertical and horizontal) with locking handles are used to adjust holding positions of the carton in the magazine holder. Vertical plates should be adjusted just enough to support the top edge of the carton (do not over support). Adjust the carton flap holders next and support the two outside flaps just enough only as shown in the 3rd picture below.

 

Once all the handles are locked, manually push the carton from bottom slightly and make sure the carton is not binding against any of the holder plates. Add more cartons now and give some weight and jiggle around to check for any binding. Cartons should move freely between the magazine holder side walls and all the holder/support brackets.

e.       Adjust the bracket for carton level sensor (shown below) for different sizes of cartons.

 

 

4.       Adjusting suction cups position on the carton

a.       Jog the machine slowly such that the rotary head arms are at 45 deg angle against the cartons on the accumulator, as shown below.

 

b.       Height of the accumulator or magazine holder should be adjusted using two adjuster screws located towards non-operator side of the machine. Use these screws to back out the accumulator so that the suction cups from the rotary head doesnt hit the cartons or any other support brackets. Once the rotary head is set at 45 deg angle, use these screws to adjust the height such that the center of the suction cup is 1.5 above the center fold of the carton as shown below in last 2 pictures. Use socket or wrench.

 


 

 

c.       Accumulator should be adjusted such that the suctions cups are just resting against the carton face and not pushing the cartons from its support plates.

 

5.       Adjusting the height of the rotary head

a.       Manually jog the machine until the rotary head is vertical (suction cups aligns vertically over a pocket in the main chain). Prepare an empty carton box and place underneath the suction cups of the rotary head, as shown in first picture below.

b.       On the non-operator side (behind the rotary head), there is a hex-head adjustment screw as shown in 2nd picture below. Use 3/4in wrench or socket to set the suction cups height; suction cups should touching the top of empty box. Once set, tighten the clamps.

 

 

6.       Adjusting space between lugs for different carton sizes

a.       Loosen two locking screws on the lug chain driving sprocket; sprocket is located towards the carton exit side of the cartoner. There is one sprocket on each side of the machine and same steps has to be followed for each sprocket to adjust lug positions.

 

b.       Install adjuster tool (this tool is kept locked inside the electrical cabinet of the cartoner) into the hole next to the adjuster sprocket. And turn the tool in the direction of the conveyor travel to reduce the space between the lugs. Against conveyor travel will increase the spacing. Use 3/4in socket or wrench.

c.       Make the adjustment in small increment until the lug barely touches the carton side. Carton should slide easily between the lugs.

d.       Make sure to tighten the locking screws once adjustment is complete. And to remove the adjuster tool.

 

7.       Adjusting the position of the erector bumper

a.       There are two erector bumpers just below the rotary head. Each bumper is held in position by 2 screws. Loosen both the screws using 1/2in wrench and adjust the position of the bumper based on size of the carton. Distance between leading edge of the lug and leading edge of the bumper should be half the size of the box side (height). So, if the cartoner is being set for 15 ct box which has 3in side, the distance should be adjusted to 1.5in. Follow the same steps for both the bumper and tighten the screws.

 

 

 

 


8.       Adjust carton top alignment guide rods and or carton top squaring bar

a.       Location of the carton top alignment guide rod is shown in picture above. It basically helps to keep the carton top flat as the suction cups drops the carton in between the lugs.

b.       This guide rod can be adjusted vertically and horizontally using two ratchet clamps as shown in the picture below. There is 2nd set of the clamps on opposite end of the rod. Make another empty carton to place underneath the guide rod housing on opposite side so that the rod and housing (or carton top squaring bar) can be adjusted equally on both ends.

 

Adjust the rod vertically to barely touch the top of the carton and horizontally just enough that it just guides and keeps the carton top flat. Do not over adjust and avoid too much pressure on top of the cartons. After the adjustment, lock the ratchet clamps and make sure to move the carton manually to check for tightness.

 

 

 

9.       Alignment of carton front major flap holder/opener bar

 

a.       Above picture shows location of top and bottom front major flap opener/holder. Bottom major flap opener is fixed in position and cannot be moved. Only top major flap opener can be adjusted vertically and horizontally.

 


 

b.       Picture above shows location of clamps to adjust the flap opener vertically and horizontally. 2nd set of clamps are on opposite end of the flap opener bar.

 

10.   Adjustment of rear minor flap closer cam and bar

a.       Now, in jog mode, manually start the machine and allow empty carton to be erected and advance forward between the lugs. Once the carton reaches just before the rear trailing edge minor flap closer cam (located on non-operator side), stop the line and adjust the cam plate height vertically only, as per carton flaps. Make sure not to disrupt the angle of the cam.

 

b.       Each cam plate is held in position by their own locking collar. On bigger cartons, both cam plates will push the trailing minor flap of rear of carton but on smaller cartons, only one cam plate will be utilized; 2nd plate will be moved out of the position. Once cam plates are adjusted, make sure to tighten the collar.

 

c.       Start the machine in jog mode again, and advance the carton forward and make sure the rear minor flaps closes without any issue.

 

d.       There is also a rear leading edge minor flap closer bar just after the cam which closes the leading flap of the carton. This bar can be adjusted horizontally and vertically, and will need to be adjusted such that the bar makes contact towards the middle section of the leading flap of the carton. While adjusting this bar make sure to check that there is no interference with the trailing edge minor flap closer cam.

 

 

 

 

11.   Adjustment of front minor flap closer cam and bar

a.       Advance the erected carton forward in jog mode until the front major flaps comes out of the major flap opener bar.

b.       Adjust the front trailing edge minor flap closing cams and front leading edge minor flap closing bar in the same way described before under #10.

 

12.   Adjustment of front and rear guide bars for carton

a.       There is one guide bar on each end of a carton (front and rear), holding the top of the carton as the minor flaps are being closed as listed under #11 above. The holding bar helps to keep carton sides square and flat.

b.       Each guide bars can be individually adjusted vertically and horizontally using locking clamps/handles. And there are two sets of clamps for each guide rod; one on each end of the bar.

c.       Adjust the height of the front guide bar such that it is slightly touching the top of the carton only. Same should be done on the other end of the bar and on the rear guide bar. See picture below for the guide bar and locking handles location.

 

 

13.   Adjustment of squaring conveyor (for reference only)

a.       Squaring conveyor is set in one position for 45 ct cartons only, and does not need to be adjusted when running 15 ct and 6 ct.

b.       Squaring conveyor has lugs which rotate on a conveyor and supports the carton side as the glue is being applied and, front and back major flaps are being closed. It helps to keep the carton sides square. For smaller cartons, there is no need for this support as the cartons are almost stable.

c.       If there is any need for adjustment, there are 4 locking bolts at the operator side of the machine as shown in 1st picture below and one height adjustment screw as shown in 2nd picture below (by the side of glue machine). Loosen the 4 bolts and slowly turn the adjustment screw until desired height is achieved. Once set, tighten all the locking bolts.

 


 

14.   Adjustment of front bottom major flap closing guide

a.       Front bottom major flap closing guide, as shown in above picture, may have to be adjusted based on size of the carton.

b.       Loosen ratchet clamp, shown in picture below, and adjust the closing guide position vertically for different size cartons.

c.       Lock the clamp once any adjustment is done.

 

15.   Adjustment of glue heads

a.       Glue is applied on front bottom major flap and rear bottom major flap with individual glue head for each side, as shown in picture below.

b.       Advance the carton in jog mode slowly and stop in front of the glue head. Loosen the locking screw and adjust the height of the head and hence nozzle such that the glue is applied on the space of the flaps indicated by white color. Each glue head has to be adjusted individually.

 

 

16.   Adjustment of front top and rear top flaps closing guide bars

 

a.       There is one guide bar with two adjustment handles which help close top major flap, and one set of this on each side to close front and rear top major flaps as shown in above picture.

b.       Loosen two outside locking bolts to release guide bar and adjust the height such that it starts to close the major flap once the glue is applied. The height of the guide bar is to be set where it can apply pressure on the glue location on the bottom major flap. Once set, tighten the locking bolts.

c.       Guide bar can also be adjusted horizontally and needs to be adjusted such that it applies enough pressure against the top major flap and bottom major flap with glue. Loosen two guide adjustment handles first and then adjust.

d.       Follow the same steps for adjusting rear guide bar.

 

17.   Once all the steps are completed, put the cartoner in Auto Mode and run the line. Check for any issue with cartons not getting pulled correctly, flaps closing issues, glue positions on the front and rear flaps of the cartons and also make sure that the top and bottom flaps are lined up correctly once the glue is applied.

 

18.   Change the access level back to operator by pressing 1 by following step# 1.

 

Changing machine modes and use of manual remote

 

a.       Main screen below shows the location of jog/auto button of the cartoner. Just press the button on the screen to toggle between jog and auto function. Also, the bottom section of the screen shows speed of the machine which can be changed simply pressing the screen on the bar. Picture below shows that the machine is in jog mode with speed set at 34 m/min. In auto mode, the machine speed setting should be at max which is 70 m/min.

 

b.       Manual remote need to be used to operate the cartoner in jog mode only. Picture below shows the remote with two buttons with up and down arrow. There is 2 ways the cartoner can be operated in jog mode run by picking up the cartons or just run the machine without picking up the cartons.

When the machine is in jog mode, keep pressing the green Cycle start/stop button on the main control panel few time and press the up arrow button (black arrow) once and look at the stacked lights on top left corner of the machine. Flickering green light indicates that the cartoner is ready to be jogged for indexing and picking up the cartons. Press the down arrow button (white arrow) to start the cartoner in jog mode. To start the cartoner without picking up the cartons, simply make sure that the green stacked light is not flickering, and just press the down arrow button.

 


Starting the cartoner by ignoring glue status

a.       In auto mode, glue has to be ready (temperatures on different zones reached to set value) for the cartoner to start. There is also an option to start the cartoner by ignoring the status of the glue. This option can be used when testing different section of the cartoner in auto mode not waiting for glue to heat up.

Make sure the screen is in service mode first (#1 above) and on the main screen, press SETUP button, press MACHINE button next, press HANDSHAKING button next, press MONITORING GLUE READY button next. The last picture below shows the last screen after all the buttons are pressed, which shows IGNORING GLUE STATUS. Now, go back to the main screen and proceed to start the cartoner.
