Cases are buckling or tearing on SEPARATION stroke

This is when a case is pushed down before being pushed up into the pins.

1)      Check if the caliper is too tight.

2)      Make sure that the cases are not pressing too much on the limit switches. Too much pressure there will cause separation problems.

3)      Make sure the magazine pusher air is not set above 40PSI.

4)      Check that the magazine width has not been changed. That is adjustment H, which should be about 20 in, outside to outside.

Cases are buckling or tearing on INJECTION stroke

The injection stroke is when the case is pushed up into the pins.

1)      Check the damper (K#16191) on the jawplates. Is it broken?

2)      Check belt feeder tension. With the conveyor empty, try pulling up on each of the belts from the middle. Both should only pull up about 2 inches. If it pulls farther, tension the belt.

3)      Are the jawplates set too tight? If so, adjust the jawplate sizing (adjustment F)

4)      Are the cases wet or damaged? Wet or damaged cases cannot be used.

5)      Check the pins (K#16206):

a.       Are they damaged or bent?

b.       Are they too close to the jawplate? The pin holder (K#16240) is reversible.

c.       Are there too many installed?

d.       Are they centered on the dome?

6)      Make sure the injector plate is not warped or damaged.

7)      If the separator blade is excessively worn, replace it, K# 17087

The pins are catching on the wrong side of the box

1)      Check the damper (K#16191) on the jawplates. Is it broken?

2)      Check that the jawplates are not too close.

3)      Flip the pinblocks as needed.

The pins are missing the box

1)      Make sure the jawplates are not too far apart, or too close together causing wedging

2)      Flip the pinblocks as needed

The cases fall from the pins

1)      Check the pins (K#16206):

a.       Are they damaged or bent?

b.       Are they too far from the jawplate? The pin holder (K#16240) is reversible.

c.       Are there too few installed?

d.       Are they centered on the dome?

2)      Make sure the injector stroke fully inserts the pins. Adjust the injector plate stroke as needed.

The case kicks towards the discharge during descent

1)      The rear minor flap folder is striking before the front minor flap folder. Check case length dimension C which should be 52cm from outside to outside on the minor flap folders.

2)      Make sure the speed of the minor flap folders are correct and that there is no binding on the cylinder

The case does not enter the major flap folders smoothly

1)      Check dimension D1. It should be 445mm from slot to slot, outside to outside.

2)      Make sure the jawplate is not slanted when open.

The forming head has difficulty rising/case pulls with forming head

1)      Make sure there are not too many pins inserted.

2)      Check dimension D1. It should be 445mm from slot to slot, outside to outside.

3)      Adjust the major flap folder secondary straight bar to better hold the box in position. Note that this bar must be straight.

The magazine case loader is running, but they are not being separated/limit switch error

1)      Make sure the magazine case loader belts are tight. Check that the belts are parallel, approximately 20 inches apart (outside to outside)

2)      Make sure there is enough pressure on the pusher arm.

3)      Check for obstructions to the case feed.

4)      Make sure the limit switches are reading. If they are broken, repace them, K#

5)      Adjust the limit switch sensor position to allow for good separation. Too much pressure will bend the injector plate, so avoid maxing the position.

6)      If the magazine case loader is running, but the belts are not moving at all, it may be necessary to replace the clutch and housing assembly, K# 11404 and K#11405

Machine Troubleshooting Sequence

Tape is wrinkled across top/bottom of box.

1)      Loosen unwind tension at tape roll mandrel.

2)      Check main tape tension adjustment at the one‐way clutch roller, point D. Roller rotation must not be too tight.

3)      Check that the main contact (wipe‐down) roller tension spring force is not too high.

4)      Check that front contact roller turns freely. Both contact rollers should turn easily but not spin loosely. If they do not turn freely, clean out the tape buildup from the roller.

Lengthways wrinkling resulting in poor coverage & poor adhesion.

1)      The box is possibly too high above the tape head. The tape head side plates should be approximately 1/8 or less away from the box. Check if the case is pulling from the forming head and follow those troubleshooting instructions.

2)      Tape threading. Check that tape is threaded and centered correctly. VERY often, the operator threads the tape wrong.

3)      Loosen unwind tension at tape roll mandrel.

4)      Check main tape tension adjustment at the one‐way clutch roller. Roller rotation must not be too tight.

The tape end is folded over on itself (it is stuck sticky side to sticky side)

1)      Check the threading of the tape.

2)      Loosen unwind tension at tape roll mandrel.

3)      Check main tape tension adjustment at the one‐way clutch roller. Roller rotation must not be too tight.

4)      Contact (wipe‐down) roller drag brake may not be working properly. Check that front contact roller turns freely. Both contact rollers should turn easily but not spin loosely.

No tape applied to box

1)      If the tape is folding over on itself

a.       Make sure the tape form is not too worn. Replace the form if it is worn or broken.

b.       Make sure the spring loaded form finger is holding the tape in place, approximately 1/8 from the form.

c.       Set the form to so that the tape shape is holding and not breaking at the contact (wipe-down) roller.

d.       Shorten the tape leg length adjustment.

2)      There is too much tension on the tape

a.       Check the threading on the tape

b.       Check if any rollers are binding and not turning freely (except the one way clutch roller)

c.       Loosen the unwind tension at tape roll mandrel

d.       Check the main tape tension at the one-way clutch roller

The tape is not cutting

1)      Check that the blade is clean and that the wipedown pad is both present and clean.

2)      Knife blade condition. Check that blade is sharp.  Replace if necessary.  Even minor damage can cause the blade to stop working.

3)      Blade may not be sufficiently extended as mounted in its holder. Slots in the blade allow its position to be adjusted but it must not interfere with blade guard operation

4)      The box is possibly too high above the tape head. The tape head side plates should be approximately 1/8 or less away from the box. Check if the case is pulling from the forming head and follow those troubleshooting instructions.

5)      Check that the springs are all in good condition.

6)      Increase the tension at the 1 way tensioner roller.

7)      Soft box quality.  If so, lessen contact roller pressure by adjusting spring force. Check that the main wipe‐down roller tension spring force is not too high.
