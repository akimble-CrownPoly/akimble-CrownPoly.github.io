Case Sealer troubleshooting guide:

Symptom:

Check

Action

Machine stop cycling while case stays at pressing area

Is the bundle too high?

During first press, the compress cylinder must reach bottom sensor.

Ask production team to adjust bundle setting to reduce bundle thickness. Getting rid of overlapping handles should solve this problem most of the time.

Is the cylinder sensor lighting up at the bottom of the stroke?

(not recommended) Adjust the bottom sensor on compress 1 cylinder about 5mm higher. Remember to mark the original position.

Case doesn’t travel forward after both major flaps close

Is one of the actuators not moving fast enough?

The speed can be adjusted by adjusting the exhaust flow on the solenoid that controls the actuator.

Is the conveyor belt closed?

 Make sure there are no debris preventing the conveyor from closing all the way to the box. Clear debris and grease the rail if there is a hinderance.

Is the conveyor belt loose?

Tighten the conveyor belt as needed.

Is the case overfilled?

Ask the operator on the line that the case was made to adjust the handle folding. Make sure the problem goes away after correction.

Is the conveyor belt trying to move, but can’t? (try pushing the belt in the direction of travel)

Reduce the pressure on the opening and closing cylinders, then actuate the cylinders to relieve the pressure.

Machine stops cycling after sealing 1 case. Amber light is flashing.

Are any of the tape head sensors loose?

Check for any loose sensors and make sure they don’t get triggered by something else.

 

Is the tape low?

If the tape is low, have the operator replace the tape.

Is the outfeed sensor reading?

The tape head outfeed sensor is a NC type sensor. The wiring on this sensor tends to twist and short, so make sure the signal is present on the PLC.

Is the tape wheel sensor switching

Make sure the laser is on the zebra wheel. Clean the wheel if dirty. Teach the sensor if needed. To teach, hold the “teach” button for 3 seconds, align the dark stripe with the sensor and press “teach”, then align the light stripe and press “teach”. Confirm switching.

Cases do not enter sealer

Are the sensors in the prepresser activating?

Clean all sensors. In the prepresser/rejector. They may be dirty and not detecting the cases

Is the rejector rejecting the case?

Check the rejector HMI to see if the case is being rejected from the case sealer and why.

Is the top sensor on the main press cylinder activated?

Make sure the main press cylinder is in the up position and that the sensor is detecting. Move the position if needed.

Is the outfeed case sensor on the conveyor activating?

Shift the outfeed case sensor position to line up with the retroreflector.

Replace if needed.

Sealer goes into error (red beacon) after 1.5-2 cases

Is the outfeed sensor reading?

Keep in mind, the outfeed sensor is a NC type. It is likely not detecting the case leaving.

Boxes Jam in the Middle of the Tape Head

Are the boxes damp?

Damp boxes cannot be used in the sealer. Have the packer close these by hand.

Are the bundles too low?

Test adjusting the bundles to increase the height. If this fixes the problem, ask the operator to adjust the folding on the line.

Is the tape head spring too tight?

Adjust the spring to lessen the tension and reduce the pressure on the box.

Is the tape head too low (pressing down into the box)?

Report this issue to an engineer.

The tape head is not cutting the tape.

Is the blade dull?

Replace and/or flip the blade. The beveled edge should be away from the head entrance.

Is the blade dry or covered in adhesive buildup?

If the felt strip is missing replace it. If the felt pad is dry, apply lubricating oil (any)

Is there enough tension on the tape?

Increase the tension on the one way tensioner roller.

Is the cutter spring stretched?

Replace the cutter spring if there is insufficient force in the cutting action.

Tape does not apply to the case.

Is the tape threaded correctly?

Follow the diagram on the sealer or the paths painted on the tape head.

Is the tape tension too high?

Reduce the tension on the one way tensioner roller.

Are all the idler rollers spinning freely?

The idler rollers tend to fill up with adhesive. Remove the tape head and disassemble to clean the shafts of the idler rollers. Lubricate with the white food safe grease and return for use on the sealer.
