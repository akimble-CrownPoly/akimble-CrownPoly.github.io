Prepresser troubleshooting guide

 

Boxes stop before entering the prepresser. 1

The conveyor does not stop when a box is at the stop plate. 1

The Prepresser cylinder does not go down or only goes down once. 1

Every case is being rejected. 1

Cases do not enter the sealer. 1

 

 

Boxes stop before entering the prepresser

1)      Check if the boxes are catching on the guide rails. If so, adjust the rails to align the box before the prepresser.

2)      The conveyor belt may be slipping. If it is, replace the belt.

3)      The sensor for the conveyor section after might be incorrectly reading high. Adjust or replace this sensor as needed. Make sure that the sensor is not reading it’s reflection on the polycarbonate paneling.

4)      If the laser sensor is reading that the press is down when it is not, then the conveyor will not push the box forward. Check the laser sensor connection. Reteach the sensor as needed.

5)      The driven conveyor roller might not be working properly. Check the electrical connections. If they are excessively twisted, it is possible that the roller itself needs replacement. Otherwise, the most likely case is that the Interroll controller needs replacement.

The conveyor does not stop when a box is at the stop plate

1)      The sensor might not be detecting the box. Increase the sensitivity, and if needed, replace the sensor.

2)      Check the dip switches on the Interroll controller card. They might be set incorrectly.

3)      The Interroll controller card may be bad. Test the card offline and replace as needed.

The Prepresser cylinder does not go down or only goes down once

1)      Make sure the E-stop is not activated

2)      Check that the laser sensor is reading correctly. Clean the tape that the laser is pointing to. Make sure that the cable connection is secure. Reteach the sensor as needed.

Every case is being rejected

1)      Check the folding. Work with the operator to get the bundle height down.

2)      Make sure that the cases are not all too big. Check the bundle count.

3)      Check that the laser sensor is reading correctly. Clean the tape that the laser is pointing to. Make sure that the cable connection is secure. Reteach the sensor as needed.

Cases do not enter the sealer

1)      Make sure that the sensors in the rejector area of the presser are reading okay. Clean them, adjust and replace as needed.

2)      Check if the rejector is working. Turn off the pneumatics and push on the actuator to check for binding. If there is binding check for where the binding occurs as it may not be the cylinder itself. With the pneumatics back on, check that the sensors on the cylinder are in position and are reading okay. If not, adjust and replace as needed.

3)      Check that the top sensor on the main press for the case sealer is activating. Move the position as needed.

4)      Make sure that the outfeed sensor for the case sealer is reading correctly. Align, clean and replace the sensor as needed.
