2)          Adjust the position to strike near the perforation

Mechanic Troubleshooting Steps

SOB is NOT Homing

1)      Check that the Drive is receiving power and that the SOB breaker is on.

2)      If the wheel is continuously turning at slow speed (50rpm) then the signal from the home sensor above the SOB is not making it to the drive. Check the sensor, cable connection and drive connection.

3)      Power cycle the drive at the drive breaker. It is common that the Apps module will not update all parameters unless cycled.

4)      If the red light is flashing on the drive, this indicates an error. Contact an engineer immediately.

SOB Homes, Then Stops

1)      The pulse signal is missing. The pulse sensor is on the non-op side attached to the perf blade shaft. Check that the sensor is close enough to read, the sensor is reading, the connection and the signal on the drive itself.

SOB Nips are NOT Closing

1)      Check the solenoid condition and connection.

2)      The cylinder that actuates the nip could be binding. Actuate it quickly to try to break it loose, and check for any mechanical interference.

3)      All units have either a physical switch, or an interlock with the winder auto signal. On units with a nip switch, check that the switch is working.

4)      On unit with a winder interlock, make sure that the winder is in auto, the relay is working, and there are no loose wires on the winder side connection. Typically the winder side connection is to the autopack signals, white and purple wires.

SOB Nips are Opening and Closing on Their Own

1)      Check for line speed fluctuations at the bag machine. This could be an issue with any of the dancers before the SOB unit.

2)      Check for a faulty solenoid and/or solenoid connection.

3)      Check for excessive play in the SOB wheel. If there is excessive play, stop the line to replace worn parts between the motor and wheel.

SOB is Working, but NOT Opening

1)      Confirm that all operator troubleshooting steps have been followed.

2)      Replace the pads and redo all operator troubleshooting steps. If needed, apply the latex veneer, K#19747

3)      Use a smart card to copy all the parameters from a working line of approximately the same speed to the line that is not working. CTsoft software can be used instead of a smart card.

SOB is Inconsistent

1)       Confirm that all operator troubleshooting steps have been followed. It is common that more pressure can be added than the operators think.

2)       Use a smart card to copy the drive parameters from a working line. If the drive parameters are off, the position can become unstable.

3)      Carefully sand, or otherwise true the SOB pad. Carefully can not be stressed enough. Often, mechanics will sand the pad unevenly leading to a weak or uneven SOB strike. The BEST way to sand the pad is on the lathe.

4)      Check the condition of the anvil rollers. If damaged or eccentric, stop the line and replace the rollers.

5)      Replace the pads and redo all operator troubleshooting steps. If needed, apply the latex veneer, K#19747

 SOB is Breaking the Film

1)      Check for hot seals. Typically the film breaking at the SOB is actually the film ripping due to hot seals.

2)      Make sure there is not too much pressure on the SOB

3)      Check the position of the SOB. Make sure it is not striking on the perf

4)      Look at the condition of the anvil roller. If there are high and low spots, the line needs to be stopped to replace these.
