Operator Troubleshooting Steps

 

1)      Clean the pads with rubbing alcohol and a scouring pad

2)      Adjust the position to strike near the perforation

3)      Increase the pressure on the SOB

4)      If bag is welding to itself at perforation, rotate perf blade.

5)      Call a mechanic.

Encoder troubleshooting steps. 1

 

 

The SOB is not starting

1)      Make sure auto-start is toggled on.

2)      Check that the product length is the length of the bag in millimeters.

3)      Verify that the speed reading is steady. If not, follow the encoder troubleshooting steps below.

4)      Make sure that the perf sensor is reading. For the CMD bag machines, this means that the knife must be on.

5)      The toggle selector for the perf reference should be set so that either option is fine, but this might have been missed on install. Test the perf signal reference selector toggle switch in both possible positions.

6)      Open the Auto start page and make sure that the minimum speed for auto-start is 50mpm.

SOB Nips are NOT Closing

1)      Check the solenoid condition and connection.

2)      The cylinder that actuates the nip could be binding. Actuate it quickly to try to break it loose and check for any mechanical interference.

3)      It is possible that SOB nip pressure adjustment is maxed, making it look like the nips are not working when they are. Check for this.

SOB is Working, but NOT Opening

1)      Confirm that all operator troubleshooting steps have been followed.

2)      Replace the pads and redo all operator troubleshooting steps. If needed, apply the latex veneer, K#19747. Also keep in mind that these new units sometimes take much more pressure to work.

3)      Verify that the product length is correct.

4)      Open the CAM profile adjustment settings and make sure the settings match the following (some options not visible if not logged in)

a.       Tool sweep overspeed: 35-45% (Note: higher means a more aggressive SOB and too high will break the film)

b.       Tool diameter: 120.00mm

c.       Tool linear length: 10.0

d.       Tool sweep length: 45.0

e.       Comp acc. %: 41.0%

f.        Comp. dec %: 25.0%

SOB is Inconsistent

1)      Confirm that all operator troubleshooting steps have been followed. It is common that more pressure can be added than the operators think.

2)      Replace the pads and redo all operator troubleshooting steps. If needed, apply the latex veneer, K#19747

3)      Check the condition of the anvil rollers. If damaged or eccentric, stop the line and replace the rollers.

4)      Verify that there are not major fluctuations in line speed. If so, identify and address the source.

5)      Verify that the product length is correct.

6)      Verify that the anvil rollers are true with the SOB pads. If not, adjust the anvil rollers to be true to the aluminum SOB pad (see Alexander).

 SOB is Breaking the Film

1)      Check for hot seals. Typically the film breaking at the SOB is actually the film ripping due to hot seals.

2)      Make sure there is not too much pressure on the SOB

3)      Check the position of the SOB. Make sure it is not striking on the perf

4)      Verify that the product length is correct

5)      Look at the condition of the anvil roller. If there are high and low spots or rust, the line needs to be stopped to replace these.

6)      In the configuration settings, make sure that the encoder pulse count is set to 1000 and the encoder wheel circumference is set as follows:

a.       For 18 inch ROM bag machine: 731.5mm

b.       For 20 inch ROM bag machine: 812.8mm

c.       For 28 inch ROM bag machine: 948.3mm

d.       For CMD bag machine: 338.6mm

7)      The SOB may not be homed. Follow the SOB homing procedure below.

Encoder troubleshooting steps

1)       A red led on the encoder (besides during startup) indicates a fault which should be troubleshot on the electrical side.

2)       If there is a green dot, check that the encoder is tightened down on the adapter shaft, and that the adapter shaft is tight to the drive shaft.

3)       If there is a red dot, power cycle the SOB unit

4)       If there are no dots on the encoder, check the power cable connections on both ends. Replacement cable is K#19661

5)       Plug the encoder (green cable) into the ethernet network to verify communication.

b.       If the dip switches are wrong, correct them and power cycle the SOB

c.       If the dip switches are correct, gently try moving the connector to the other port.

d.       Run a new encoder cable directly from the network to the encoder to verify if the issue is in the cable, K#19662.

e.       If none of the above steps work, replace the encoder, K#19906

SOB Homing Procedure.

The sob home position is not automatically updated, and it must be done manually. Here are the steps to do this.

1)      Stop the SOB

2)      Log in and open the configuration menu (top right menu)

3)      Tap the homing icon:

4)      This icon represents the calibration position:

5)      Loosen the cam bolt ring.

6)      Position the SOB shaft so that the very tip of the pad is about to touch the anvil as shown in the icon.

7)      Rotate the cam bolt ring about the shaft until the edge triggers the homing sensor. Once it is in position, tighten the ring to secure it.

8)      Close the guard and reset the safety.

9)      Press the Teach Home button on the screen.

10)  Wait for the homing to complete.

11)  Once complete, double check that when the front edge of the pad is about to touch the anvil the degrees is close to 0 or 360.

12)  Sign out.

13)  Back off the SOB adjustment so that the nip is not touching the anvil.

14)  Start the SOB.

15)  Have the operator adjust the SOB position, then apply pressure.
