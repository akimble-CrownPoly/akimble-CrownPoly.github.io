T2 Glue Troubleshooting Guide

 

Glue is not coming out of any of the 4 nozzles. 1

Glue is not coming out of 1 or 2 nozzles. 1

The glue position is moving. 2

 

 

Glue is not coming out of any of the 4 nozzles

1)      Is the hot melt unit up to temperature? Open the tank and check if glue is melted. The display should show a reading of 290F

2)      Is the controller on? Make sure the controller is on.

3)      Are the settings correct? The event 01 setting should be about 26, and the event 02 setting should be about 0.5. The setup should be 01. The sensor input for 1 and 2 valve group should be 1, and the sensor input for 3 and 4 valve group should be 2.

4)      Is the pulse signal present on the controller? If not, check the solid state relays.

5)      Is the encoder turning? Check the controller menu to see if there is a reading from the encoder. Push down on the middle green switch to view the read line speed on the controller.

6)      Check the mechanical relay in the winder. If it not triggering (faint audible click) replace it K#16922.

7)      If the tachometer was just replaced, check to make sure it is rotating in the correct direction. The shaft is required to rotate in a counterclockwise direction, so if incorrect, swap the least on the tach generator.

 

Glue is not coming out of 1 or 2 nozzles

1)      Are the nozzle and tube hot? Check to make sure the heater elements are plugged in. If not plug them in.

2)      Manually activate the glue using the bypass on the solenoid. See if that is capable of coming out. If not, check for a clog. The bypass is the inset blue button on the solenoid. It can be pressed with something like a pen.

3)      If it worked on the bypass solenoid, try manually activating on the controller. If this does not work, check for damaged wires or a loose connection.

4)      Use a slow motion camera to make sure that the glue is actually not firing, not just missing the bag. If it is missing the bag, reduce the length to fire setting.

5)      Check the glue color. If the color is a thick honey like orange, purge the glue.

6)      Make sure the film is traveling under the spark unit. Fix this by moving the film, not the spark unit.

7)      Check that the solid state relays are lighting up in accordance with the spark unit. If the spark unit is indicating spark, but the relays are not lighting up, replace the relay.

8)      Check the relay internal to the control panel. Replace this relay, or if needed, replace the board.

 

The glue position is moving

1)      Is the encoder wheel riding on the film? This is necessary to get a good reading of line speed. If the encoder is on the roller, it will give a poor reading due to slip between the roller and encoder. If the roller that the encoder was on was moved, this will also cause the same problem.

Push down on the middle green switch to view the read line speed on the controller.

2)      Is the time long enough for the glue to shoot? Insufficient time for the glue air solenoid will prevent the glue leaving the nozzle with a consistent time. The time should be set to at least 0.4.

3)      Are there air bubbles in the glue? Purge the air from the glue.

4)      Is the air pressure set to 75PSI? See the picture below. The dial indicator on the left is the pump pressure. Putting this too high will blow out the pump, so leave it at 40PSI. The right indicator is the gun pressure, which should be set to 75PSI.

5)      Is the nozzle temperature high enough? It should be about 290F.
